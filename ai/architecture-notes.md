# Architecture Notes

Last updated: 2026-06-19

## Current Architecture

- The project is a Unity 2D/URP project with a thin runtime-generated MVP strategy layer.
- `StrategyGameBootstrap` runs after scene load, creates/wires the map, strategy wind, visual day/night cycle, runtime post-processing, cinematic visuals, ambience audio, forestry, population, nature props, and camera without scene YAML dependencies, focuses the initial camera view on the startup campfire once population startup has created it, and configures nature after population so the camp clear radius can be excluded.
- `StrategyDebugLogger` is created before other strategy systems, writes structured session lines to project-root `debug.log` in the Unity Editor, mirrors Unity log messages, and exposes static `Info`/`Warn`/`Error` helpers for gameplay events.
- `StrategyRuntimeObjectCreationGuard` blocks loose-resource pile factories from creating new scene GameObjects while Play Mode is shutting down or when runtime code is not actually playing.
- `CityMapController` owns basic map-cell data, randomizes an active generation seed by default, derives a terrain-generation profile from that seed, tags water/shore cells by `CityMapWaterKind.River` or `CityMapWaterKind.Lake`, exposes `RiverFlowDirection` for current-following systems, and renders the current map as a generated point-filtered texture on a `SpriteRenderer`.
- `StrategyTerrainTexturePainter` paints the generated map texture with active-seed 16px pixel-art variants for Grass, Meadow, Forest, Dirt, Shore, and Water, including neighbor-aware side/corner transition overlays.
- `StrategyWaterAnimationController` renders one generated point-filtered overlay texture above the static map for animated water depth tint, directional river streaks, lake sparkles, broken shoreline foam, wet shore-edge pixels, and rain/storm ripple highlights.
- `StrategyTrailController` tracks runtime activity-weighted resident footfall wear on walkable/buildable land cells, decays stale wear after unused cells age past a grace period, renders visual trails under a stricter cardinal-only `Trail Visuals` root, refreshes nearby trail visuals when map walkability/buildability changes, and exposes functional formed trails as a 15% resident speed multiplier plus reduced pathfinding cost independent of visual-only filtering.
- `StrategyTrailSpriteFactory` generates connected procedural pixel-art trail sprites for cardinal N/E/S/W right-angle neighbor masks, 3 wear levels, and deterministic variants.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` is created after map generation and places trees, forest groups, and bushes over generated terrain using `CityMapController.ActiveSeed` and cell kinds; these props mark their occupied cells as not walkable.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` supports a cell-radius exclusion and currently skips generated nature props within 3 cells of the startup campfire.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` also places generated Stone deposits before vegetation on a cell: standalone Boulders, Rock Clusters, and larger Cliffs that register with `StrategyStoneResourceController` and block their occupied footprints.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` places generated underground Iron fields as multi-cell flat rust-stained ground and shallow vein markings on walkable land; they register with `StrategyIronResourceController`, remain walkable, block normal building placement through the map buildability overlay, avoid adjacent Coal fields, and can be mined by Mine worksites built over them.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` places generated underground Coal fields as multi-cell flat dark dust ground and coal seam markings on walkable land; they register with `StrategyCoalResourceController`, remain walkable, block normal building placement through the map buildability overlay, avoid adjacent Iron fields, and can be mined by Coal Pit worksites built over them.
- `StrategyNaturePropController` places generated near-water Clay fields as multi-cell wet clay patches/banks on walkable shore/land cells within a short water radius; they register with `StrategyClayResourceController`, remain walkable, block normal building placement, avoid adjacent Iron/Coal/Clay fields, and can be mined by Clay Pit worksites built over them.
- When the startup campfire exclusion center is known, `StrategyNaturePropController` guarantees at least 5 starter-area Stone deposits within `StrategyStonecutterCamp.WorkRadius`, outside the camp clear radius and with adjacent walkable work cells.
- `StrategyForageResourceController`, `StrategyForageNode`, and `StrategyForageSpriteFactory` create non-blocking Berries/Roots/Mushrooms forage nodes on suitable walkable land after nature generation, guarantee a small starter forage ring, support resident reservation/depletion/regrowth, and provide ready/depleted node plus carried basket sprites.
- `StrategyWildlifeController` runs after starter placement and fog/placement setup, builds lake fish regions and a river route from generated `CityMapWaterKind` cells, and spawns deer herds, rabbit groups, wolf packs, capped lake fish shoals, one-way pass-through river fish, and ambient birds only in currently hidden daylight-range fog cells within a broad ring around completed buildings or active construction sites; the startup camp is a fallback anchor only when no building/construction anchor exists. `StrategyWildlifeController.Part11.cs` owns the shared hidden-near-settlement candidate checks used by initial spawn, lake/river fish spawn, reproduction birth cells, and migration targets. Land wildlife travel avoids placed buildings, active construction sites, and the campfire within a 4-cell structure buffer while still allowing generated River crossings as transit-only path cells. Wolf pack placement prefers alternating river sides when a generated river route exists, and wolf prey lookup only reserves rabbit/deer surplus above high control thresholds after subtracting active predator reservations and hunter-reserved rabbits. `StrategyWildlifeRiverCrossing` separates land target cells from land-or-River travel cells: deer, rabbits, and wolves can path through generated River cells with slowed swimming movement and ripple visuals, but final path targets and hunter/wolf prey reservations remain on land cells without changing global map walkability or allowing Lake crossings. `StrategyDeerAgent` owns deer sex/life stage, fawn growth, predator reservation, state transitions, local pathing, resident/noisy-work threat checks, swimming crossings, threat-reaction throttling, and frame-based idle/walk/graze/alert/flee/rest animation; `StrategyRabbitAgent` owns the matching rabbit behavior set plus hunter reservation, predator reservation, arrow-hit, missed-arrow flee reaction, death, carcass, butchering-ready states, swimming crossings, and threat-reaction throttling; `StrategyWolfAgent` owns pack roaming, settlement-pressure avoidance, threshold-limited rabbit/deer hunting with a short fast pounce phase, swimming crossings, and frame-based idle/walk/run/stalk/attack/eat/howl animation; `StrategyFishAgent` owns lake-vs-river habitat state, water-cell pathing, river-route following/despawn, fry growth, swimming, feeding, turning, dart/flee behavior, fishing reservation, hooked/caught states, and surface ripple visuals; `StrategyBirdAgent` owns decorative bird state, habitat landing choices, flying/fleeing, pecking, hopping, duck swimming, and flight shadows.
- `StrategyStoneResourceController` / `StrategyStoneDeposit` track runtime Stone resource nodes with deposit kind, footprint, remaining Stone amount, and reservation hooks for future worker jobs.
- `StrategyIronResourceController` / `StrategyIronDeposit` track runtime underground Iron indicators with deposit kind, footprint, remaining Iron amount, reservation state, and mining hooks for `StrategyMine`.
- `StrategyCoalResourceController` / `StrategyCoalDeposit` track runtime underground Coal indicators with deposit kind, footprint, remaining Coal amount, reservation state, and mining hooks for `StrategyCoalPit`.
- `StrategyClayResourceController` / `StrategyClayDeposit` track runtime near-water Clay fields with deposit kind, footprint, remaining Clay amount, reservation state, inspect data, buildability blocking, and mining hooks for `StrategyClayPit`.
- `StrategyForestryController` / `StrategyForestryTree` register generated standalone trees as mature forestry targets, block tree cells while trees exist, support hit-driven chopping, animate tree shake/cut marks/falling, keep fallen trunks as bucking targets, split them into pickup Logs, release walkability after Logs are taken, and plant saplings that grow through 3 visual stages. Small trees yield 3 Logs, while large generated trees and planted mature trees yield 6 Logs.
- `StrategyWindController` creates/configures a Unity directional `WindZone` as the runtime wind source, accepts weather influence boosts, and `StrategyWindSway` adapts the wind values into 2D sprite sway for generated trees, forest groups, and bushes.
- `StrategyWeatherController` owns the runtime weather state, randomly transitions between Clear, Cloudy, LightRain, HeavyRain, Fog, and Storm, exposes smooth rain/cloud/fog/storm/wind/wetness intensities, boosts the strategy wind source, feeds rain/wind ambience, and lets water animation intensify ripple hits during rain.
- `StrategyWeatherVisualController` renders procedural wet-ground, cloud-shadow, heavy-rain mist, and rain overlays in dedicated sorting bands around day/night, preview, and fog-of-war layers; dense Fog weather rendering is masked through fog-of-war instead of covering visible cells with a uniform mist overlay.
- `StrategyAmbientAudioController` loads non-generated nature loops from `Assets/Resources/Audio/Nature`, creates runtime ambience sources, follows map water cells with a spatial river source, and blends forest birds/cicadas/night/rain/wind based on time, map forest density, strategy wind values, and active weather.
- `StrategyDayNightCycleController` creates a runtime full-map sprite overlay sorted above world sprites and below preview/fog/UI, blends dawn/day/dusk/night tint colors over a 300-second scaled-time loop, exposes a `StrategyCalendarSnapshot` with display day, 24-hour clock, time-of-day phase, and phase accent color, sends dawn/nightfall messages to the event log, and blends the camera background color with the same phase without adding gameplay effects.
- `StrategyFogOfWarController` renders runtime fog over the generated map, keeps persistent explored cells separate from current visibility, reduces camp/resident/building reveal radii during Dusk/Night/Dawn from the shared calendar phase, further reduces those radii during dense Fog weather, darkens explored-but-not-visible cells at night, replaces explored gray zones with light/medium/dense weather-fog bands during Fog weather while leaving visible cells clear, and keeps a daylight-range visibility mask for spawn systems that should ignore temporary sight loss.
- `StrategyDebugPanelController` owns the F9 runtime debug overlay: it blocks world input while open, lets testing disable/enable player fog through `StrategyFogOfWarController.SetPlayerFogEnabled()`, toggles shared `StrategyDebugOptions` such as instant free construction, and forces weather states through `StrategyWeatherController.ForceWeatherSmooth()` so debug switching uses the normal gradual atmospheric transition.
- `StrategyPostProcessController` creates a runtime global URP Volume, enables post-processing on the strategy camera, and blends readable dawn/dusk/night color grading, bloom, and vignette from day/night phase plus weather rain/cloud/fog/storm/wetness intensities.
- `StrategyCinematicVisualController` reuses or creates one runtime 2D global light, scans completed buildings including Bridges plus the campfire for reusable `StrategyCinematicLightEmitter` attachments, renders a low-resolution camera-space nighttime darkness mask with soft light cutouts around active emitters, renders wet puddle glints and subtle foreground depth props around the active camera, and adds randomized rain/storm lightning flashes with occasional multi-flash bursts. It applies camera-distance LOD, caps active point lights, and updates darkness/puddles/foregrounds on short intervals rather than every frame. `StrategyCinematicLightEmitter` lazily creates local 2D point lights only when selected by the LOD budget, while emissive pixel masks and animated torch/lantern/brazier/bridge-lamp sprites keep distant night light sources visible cheaply.
- `StrategyShadowCaster2D` and `StrategyShadowSpriteFactory` provide shared procedural soft/cast ground shadows for runtime world sprites; day/night exposes global shadow opacity and length multipliers while each object type tunes its own shape, scale, offset, and sorting offset.
- `StrategyMusicController` loads all AudioClips from `Assets/Resources/Audio/Music` as an in-game playlist, picks random tracks, avoids repeating the previous track when 2+ tracks exist, and pauses/resumes the current clip on application focus loss without advancing the playlist.
- `StrategyResidentFootstepAudio` loads non-generated grass footsteps from `Assets/Resources/Audio/Footsteps/GrassWalk` and is attached by `StrategyResidentAgent` to play quiet spatial footstep one-shots on selected walk animation frames.
- `StrategyTimeScaleController` is runtime-created by bootstrap and maps F1/F2/F3 plus Build HUD x1/x2/x3 buttons to simulation speed by updating `Time.timeScale` and `Time.fixedDeltaTime`; modal gameplay systems use its pause-lock API so they can pause simulation without losing the requested speed.
- `StrategyNatureSpriteFactory` generates and caches procedural 2.5D pixel-art sprites for larger large trees, small trees, bushes, forest groups, Stone boulders/clusters/cliffs, walkable Iron-stained ground/vein indicators, walkable Coal dust/seam indicators, walkable wet Clay patch/bank indicators, felled trunks, split Logs, carried Logs, carried Iron, carried Coal, carried Clay, carried `Game`, and carried `Fish`.
- `StrategyResourceType`, `StrategyHouseResourceStore`, `StrategyHouseResourceStore.Dishes`, `StrategyDishRecipeCatalog`, and `StrategyResourceIconFactory` define the current resource layer, including Eggs/crops/forage food, recipe-based prepared dishes, `Logs`, `Stone`, `Iron`, `Coal`, `Clay`, `Planks`, and `Pottery`, plus shared runtime HUD icons for construction, production stock, and storage resources.
- `CityMapController` also owns counted dynamic walkability and buildability overlays used by placement/population for overlapping runtime blockers plus a bridge-walkability overlay that lets completed bridge spans make River water cells walkable without changing their water kind; these overlay changes notify the trail layer to refresh affected cells.
- `StrategyCameraController` owns orthographic navigation, initial focus requests, and clamps movement to the map bounds.
- `StrategyBuildMenuController` owns the first runtime HUD: a bottom Build button, category dock, item tray, Logs/Stone/Planks construction costs, active build-tool state, resource affordability, temporary goal-driven tool locks, F9 instant-construction affordability bypass display, and full menu closing after placement; the catalog includes Infrastructure/Bridge.
- `StrategyProfessionHudController` owns the top-menu `Professions` HUD: it scans current worksite components, shows dynamic profession rows with generated icons and assigned/capacity counts, includes Miners for Mine worksites, Coal Miners for Coal Pits, Clay Diggers for Clay Pits, and Potters for Kilns, treats Storage Yard Haulers/builders as uncapped roles, exposes `Auto Assign` plus compact priority steppers, and routes player `-`/`+` assignment commands through each worksite's existing assignment API. Haulers are the single player-facing logistics profession and also fill Granaries.
- `StrategyAutoWorkforceController` is runtime-created by bootstrap and uses `StrategyAutoWorkforceSettings` plus demand records to assign eligible free adults every few real-time seconds. It keeps a throttled cached snapshot of current worksite arrays instead of refreshing every scaled simulation tick, treats Construction, Food, Logistics, Wood, Stone, Planks, Iron, Coal, Clay, and Pottery values as desired worker targets for currently workable vacancies, while shortage, urgency, worksite capacity, storage backlog, production-input backlog, construction readiness, and resident distance affect demand ordering. Each tick also computes desired profession targets, releases surplus workers from overstaffed auto-managed roles, can release limited lower-priority auto-managed donors for higher-scored shortages through existing unassign APIs, and caps successful assignments so large rebalances spread across later ticks. Manual worker removal from the Profession HUD creates a short profession override so automation does not immediately refill that role.
- `StrategyBuildPlacementController` owns MVP placement: map-cell hover preview, validation, construction-site creation, construction resource reservation, occupied cells, final placed runtime building records, expanded 2.5D walk-blocker footprints for houses and work camps, fisher-hut water/shore validation, Bridge's two-click river-bank span placement/highlighting, and tool-specific worksite component creation after completion. Normal building construction-site validation keeps the technical foundation strict, rejects map buildability blockers such as underground Iron/Coal and near-water Clay, reserves the future final 2.5D blocker without requiring all of it to be currently walkable, requires nearby builder work access, and only blocks foundation cells while the site is under construction; Mine, Coal Pit, and Clay Pit can place over their matching resource cells. F9 instant construction mode skips resource reservation but still creates and immediately completes a construction site so building completion listeners and starter goals use the same finalization path; toggling it on also completes existing active construction sites.
- `StrategyBuildPlacementController` closes the full Build menu after successful construction-site placement and marks the placement frame so world selection does not auto-select the new site from the same click.
- Bootstrap uses `StrategyBuildPlacementController.TryPlaceStarterStorageYard` to create the initial Storage Yard near the campfire through the same placed-building path as player construction.
- `StrategyPlacedBuilding` is the runtime record attached to placed buildings; it stores tool, origin, footprint, footprint bounds, selected visual variant, assigned resident references, installed visual upgrades, Bridge span cells/endpoints, exposes installed upgrade records, owns the house resource store, and has a 2D click collider.
- `StrategyConstructionSite` is the runtime bridge between placement and final buildings: it stores final tool metadata, target footprint, reserved/delivered Logs/Stone/Planks, an uncapped assigned hired-builder list, staged construction sprites, delivered material stockpiles, optional Bridge span/work cells, progress, and completion into a final placed building; it periodically requests hired builders from Storage Yards while waiting, and exposes a debug-only instant completion hook used by the F9 panel path.
- `StrategyStorageYard` is the current storage/logistics worksite: it stores uncapped local Logs, Stone, Iron, Coal, Clay, Planks, and Pottery, supports uncapped assigned Haulers plus uncapped hired builders, finds production worksites with available output stock, reserves Logs/Stone/Iron/Coal/Clay/Planks/Pottery for haulers, reserves non-food production inputs for delivery from Storage Yard stock into production nodes, exposes Pottery reservations for householder pickup, lets Haulers move `Game`/`Fish` to Granaries, reserves Logs/Stone/Planks for construction sites, provides construction pickup cells, dispatches hired builders across active construction sites with balanced empty-site/low-builder priority, and updates visible Logs/Stone/Iron/Coal/Clay/Planks/Pottery stockpiles.
- `IStrategyProductionLogisticsNode` is the shared production-chain logistics contract: production buildings expose input delivery requests and output pickup requests, while Storage Yard Haulers perform inter-building resource transfers through Storage Yard stock. Food-chain inputs should use Granary-side logistics when they are introduced.
- `StrategyProductionStorage` centralizes the current production-building local stock cap of 6 resources; production worksites use it to stop starting new work when a local buffer would overflow, while Storage Yards and Granaries stay uncapped.
- `StrategyLumberjackCamp` remains the lumber production worksite, but its capped local Logs stock can now be reserved and picked up by Haulers.
- `StrategySawmill` is the Planks production worksite and first `IStrategyProductionLogisticsNode`: it supports 1 assigned Sawyer, requests Logs as a Storage Yard delivered production input, keeps the Sawyer visible inside the building with a saw/log/plank overlay plus sawdust effects, stores capped local Logs/Planks stock with pending Planks counting against the same cap, caps input Logs at 4 to preserve output room under the shared production cap, and exposes Planks to Storage Yard haulers as output.
- `StrategyKiln` is the Pottery production worksite and an `IStrategyProductionLogisticsNode`: it supports 1 assigned Potter, requests Clay and Coal as Storage Yard delivered production inputs, keeps the Potter visible at the Kiln with a firing overlay plus spark/dust effects, stores capped local Clay/Coal/Pottery stock with pending Pottery counting against the same cap, and exposes Pottery to Storage Yard haulers as output.
- `StrategyMine` is the Iron production worksite: it requires an available underground Iron indicator under its footprint, supports up to 2 assigned Miners, keeps Miners hidden underground during active work while the entrance emits intermittent dust/spark effects, stores capped local Iron stock, and exposes that stock to Storage Yard haulers.
- `StrategyCoalPit` is the Coal production worksite: it requires an available underground Coal indicator under its footprint, supports up to 2 assigned Coal Miners, keeps workers visible inside the pit during active work with coal dust/chip effects, stores capped local Coal stock, and exposes that stock to Storage Yard haulers.
- `StrategyClayPit` is the Clay production worksite: it requires an available near-water Clay field under its footprint, supports up to 2 assigned Clay Diggers, keeps workers visible inside the pit during active work, stores capped local Clay stock, and exposes that stock to Storage Yard haulers.
- `StrategyHunterCamp` is the first hunting worksite: it supports up to 2 assigned hunters, reserves the nearest available adult rabbit on the map through `StrategyWildlifeController`, uses resident-side ranged bow stand selection with a 20% miss chance, receives capped local `Game`, and updates a local visual stockpile.
- `StrategyFisherHut` is the first fishing worksite: it supports up to 2 assigned fishers, reserves the nearest available adult fish on the map through `StrategyWildlifeController`, chooses reachable land/shore stand cells with adjacent water, keeps range validation active through cast/wait/reel phases, receives capped local `Fish`, and updates a local visual stockpile.
- `StrategyGranary` is the first food-storage worksite: it stores uncapped local raw `Game` and `Fish` ingredients, exposes settlement-level availability/reservation helpers for Householder pickup, is filled by Storage Yard Haulers, and updates separate visual food stockpiles.
- `StrategyBuildingUpgradeController` installs house upgrades near placed houses. It currently supports Garden Beds and Chicken Coop, charges optional upgrade construction costs from available Storage Yard resources, assigns produced resources to upgrades, uses map walkability only as a placement filter, does not block cells after installation, and spawns idle chickens when Chicken Coop is installed.
- `StrategyBuildingUpgradeSpriteFactory` generates and caches runtime pixel-art sprites for the current visual upgrades.
- `StrategyPopulationController` creates a startup camp with an animated procedural campfire, spawns 3 initial family blocks with a father, a mother, and 1-2 adult children each, assigns random Germanic/Nordic-style full names and age-appropriate adult ages, owns the runtime resident ID, family-record, house registry, and orphan-adoption pass, populates completed houses from whole homeless families before falling back to the homeless adult pair pool and adult-child migration/partner lookup, and funnels resident death through assignment cleanup plus funeral snapshot creation; workplace and construction roles do not prevent home assignment, and residents in active funeral duty are temporarily immune to death attempts.
- `StrategyRefugeeArrivalController` gates the first pending refugee family on 3 completed houses, schedules later families by random repeat interval, fades arrival intensity after 40 accepted residents, stops arrivals at 50 accepted residents, routes them from a walkable map edge to the campfire, pauses on arrival through `StrategyTimeScaleController`, and either registers accepted families as normal residents or sends rejected temporary agents back off-map for cleanup.
- `StrategyTopStatusHudController` is a small runtime-created top HUD layer that reads adult/child counts from `StrategyPopulationController` and calendar/time snapshots from `StrategyDayNightCycleController`; pending refugees are not counted until accepted, the compact population panel toggles `StrategyPopulationRosterHudController`, and a separate top-right widget shows day number, 24-hour clock time, phase label, phase accent, and day progress. The roster HUD lists accepted residents with settlement stats, filters, age, home/camp state, role, current status, and food status, using `StrategyResidentHudText` for shared labels with the selection HUD. The roster also opens `StrategyFamilyTreeHudController`, a fullscreen modal family-tree scene that pushes a `StrategyTimeScaleController` pause lock, uses permanent horizontal/vertical scrollbars, groups recorded members into connected same-surname family cards, lays those cards out as affinity-ordered left-to-right family columns with compact generation rows, and draws parent-child portrait-card connections plus cross-family relationship lines for living and deceased records. Deceased tree cards are muted monochrome cards with a skull marker, every member card shows a gender symbol, and hovering a card shows relationship labels on other recorded relatives. `StrategyEventLogHudController` is a separate compact top event feed for births, deaths, adoptions, dawn, and nightfall. `StrategyGoalsController` and `StrategyGoalsHudController` provide the runtime checklist layer; `StrategyStarterGoalSequenceController` seeds the first onboarding build goals and uses Build menu tool locks until the player completes 3 Houses, a Lumberjack Camp, and a Stonecutter Camp.
- `StrategyPopulationController` periodically checks empty and single-resident houses: empty houses receive the oldest adult child still living with parents, and single adult-child households try to pull in an adult opposite-gender partner from another parental home or the free camp pool while using kinship checks. When an adult male/female household pair forms, the wife takes the husband's family name and the resident family record is refreshed without rewriting biological parent/child IDs.
- `StrategyHouseholdState` lives on occupied house buildings and runs the randomized household birth timer; it asks `StrategyPopulationController` to spawn children only for adult male/female pairs that pass `StrategyKinshipUtility` close-relative checks and when the house has a free resident slot.
- `StrategyHouseholdFoodState` lives on occupied house buildings and resolves one nightly dinner per day after a one-day settling grace. Dinner waits for eligible residents to enter the house during `Night`, falls back after a short deadline if someone is stuck, sums resident age-based ration needs, consumes prepared house-local recipe dishes first, falls back to house-local ingredients for missing rations, applies short rations to each resident's nutrition debt/days hungry, and exposes aggregate food status for HUDs while keeping consumed dish/ingredient counts for context.
- Householder home duty can reserve one raw `Fish`/`Game` ingredient unit from a reachable Granary or Pottery from a reachable Storage Yard, path to pickup, carry it home, store it in the house resource store, and cook stored ingredients plus 1 Pottery into available recipes during `Dusk`.
- `StrategyResidentAgent` stores a persistent runtime full name, family name, age, life stage, parent IDs, and child ID links, drives trail-aware 8-direction A* movement through walkable map cells using `CityMapController.IsCellWalkable`, prevents diagonal corner cutting, smooths reconstructed paths through walkable line-of-sight checks, records activity-weighted visible resident footfalls into `StrategyTrailController`, owns visual-only readability child renderers for a synced silhouette outline plus ground shadow, and attaches `StrategyResidentFootstepAudio` for quiet grass footsteps on walk frames; children younger than 3 stay inside their assigned home with hidden world sprite/collider and skip outdoor idle/funeral movement, older children idle/walk near home, cannot be assigned to work/build, grow into adults at age 16 after scaled game time, continue aging after adulthood, and roll annual mortality through `StrategyPopulationController` from age 1. Resident funeral activities can temporarily interrupt work for mourning, procession, and burial while keeping workplace roles intact afterward. Assigned adult work is schedule-gated by `StrategyDayNightCycleController.IsSettlementWorkTime`: new production, construction, logistics, hunting, fishing, foraging, garden, and household-food pickup use the Dawn-through-Dusk work window; nightfall defers reached work targets, and carried resources/deposits/returns are allowed to finish to avoid stuck reservations. During the `Night` phase, housed idle residents path to their home, hide inside the house with world sprite/collider disabled, and wake at the home exit after night ends. Assigned adult residents also periodically path to their home Garden Beds, play a short work animation, and add the Garden Beds crop to the owning house when work completes. Householders can fetch raw `Fish`/`Game` from Granaries, fetch Pottery from Storage Yards, and cook stored ingredients plus Pottery into available prepared dish recipes during `Dusk`. Non-householder residents without external work can forage Berries/Roots/Mushrooms during daytime and carry that food back to their own house; children younger than 7 do not forage. Residents assigned to a lumberjack camp path to the nearest available tree/processable wood, play frame-based axe swings, send tree hit events on impact frames, wait for falling to finish, buck the fallen trunk into Logs, carry Logs to their camp stockpile, then plant saplings nearby. Residents assigned to a sawmill wait for Hauler-delivered input Logs, work visibly inside the building, saw Logs into Planks, and store them at the Sawmill. Residents assigned to a hunter camp reserve the nearest available adult rabbit, move to roughly 2-3 tile bow range, fire projectile arrows with a 20% miss chance, approach carcasses on hit, butcher them, carry `Game`, and deposit it at the camp; missed arrows stick in the ground and make the rabbit flee. Residents assigned to a fisher hut reserve the nearest available fish, move to reachable shore, cast and reel fishing lines while range remains valid, carry `Fish`, and deposit it at the hut. Storage Yard Haulers can deliver non-food production inputs from Storage Yard stock into production nodes and run raw food delivery into the nearest Granary after normal storage-resource haul checks. Residents hired as Storage Yard builders fetch reserved Logs/Stone/Planks from construction resource sources, deliver materials to construction sites, then build with frame-based hammer swings and timed progress hits.
- Clay Pit resident work follows the visible in-pit production pattern: Clay Diggers path to the pit entrance, reserve near-water Clay fields under the footprint, dig Clay while visible, add Clay to pit stock, and use carried Clay cleanup/return paths when interrupted.
- `StrategyFuneralController`, `StrategyCorpse`, `StrategyCemeteryController`, and `StrategyFuneralSpriteFactory` own the current funeral/cemetery MVP: deaths create animated corpse snapshots, family/household participants gather and cry/mourn, lonely deaths with no living funeral participants use a silent one-adult service burial fallback, the corpse is dragged by rope behind a carrier toward a spontaneous cemetery, burial waits for reachable attendees at the grave, burial creates a runtime grave sprite, and the grave cell becomes not walkable.
- Home assignment on `StrategyResidentAgent` updates the resident's family/home binding without cancelling active workplace or construction tasks; idle residents can immediately walk toward the new home, while busy residents keep their current task flow and later idle around the assigned home.
- Haulers are driven by `StrategyResidentAgent`: they reserve worksite Logs/Stone/Iron/Coal/Clay/Planks/Pottery outputs, prioritize Stone when active construction lacks yard Stone or when yard Stone is lower than Logs, walk to source worksites, pick up resources, carry them to the storage yard, deposit them, deliver non-food production inputs from Storage Yard stock into production nodes, and then can reserve `Game`/`Fish` or loose food for delivery to the nearest Granary. Householders handle the final Storage Yard/Granary to House pickup for house-bound resources.
- `StrategyCampfireSpriteFactory` and `StrategyCampfireAnimator` generate and cycle the current campfire animation frames at runtime; `StrategyCampfireAnimator` also blocks the campfire cell while burning, fades/shrinks the fire during burnout, destroys the visual object, and clears that map walkability blocker when extinguished.
- `StrategyChickenAgent` drives small idle chickens around their linked Chicken Coop using the same early local walkability approach.
- `StrategyDeerSpriteFactory` generates procedural 2.5D male/female deer sprite frames, including larger antlered bucks and smaller does, for idle breathing, walking, grazing, alert, running, and resting behaviors.
- `StrategyRabbitSpriteFactory` generates procedural 2.5D male/female rabbit sprite frames for idle, hopping, nibbling, alert, fleeing, grooming, resting, arrow hit, death, and carcass behaviors; kits reuse scaled rabbit visuals.
- `StrategyFishSpriteFactory` generates procedural fish sprite frames for Minnow, Carp, and Perch idle, swimming, dart/flee, turn, feed, and hooked/reeling behaviors; fry reuse scaled fish visuals.
- `StrategyBirdSpriteFactory` generates procedural 2.5D bird sprite frames for Sparrow, Crow, and Duck idle, pecking, hopping, flying, landing, and duck swimming behaviors.
- `StrategyBuildingSpriteFactory` generates and caches runtime building sprites in code; House has 5 larger 2.5D variants, Lumberjack Camp has 3 variants, Stonecutter Camp has 3 variants, Sawmill has 3 variants, Kiln has 3 variants, Mine has 3 variants, Coal Pit has 3 variants, Clay Pit has 3 variants, Hunter Camp has 3 variants, Fisher Hut has 3 variants, Storage Yard has 3 variants, Granary has 3 variants, and Bridge has dynamic span-sized sprites. Variant 0 is used by menu/preview and random variants are used for placed supported buildings.
- `StrategyResidentSpriteFactory` generates and caches 5 male and 5 female adult resident sprites, child resident sprites, walk frames, woodcut axe-swing frames, stonecut pickaxe frames, bow-shot frames, butchering frames, fishing cast/reel frames, construction hammer/build frames, and matching portrait sprites at runtime.
- `StrategyBuildingSpriteFactory` now also generates worksite/storage resource stockpile sprites for Logs, Stone, Iron, Coal, Clay, Planks, Pottery, `Game`, and `Fish`.
- `StrategyConstructionSpriteFactory` generates staged construction-site sprites; delivered construction material stockpiles use the shared material stockpile sprite generators.
- `StrategyWoodcutEffectAnimator` generates short procedural woodchip/leaf/dust hit effects for tree chopping.
- `StrategyWorldEffectAnimator` generates reusable short-lived world effects for dust, sawdust, stone/coal chips, iron sparks, water splashes, and resource pop/fade moments; it uses `StrategyRuntimeObjectCreationGuard` so effects are not spawned during runtime shutdown.
- `StrategyChickenSpriteFactory` generates and caches the current runtime chicken sprite.

## Current Hotspots

- Runtime bootstrap is global for every loaded scene. Future menus/multi-scene flow may need an explicit boot scene or mode gate.
- Map generation/rendering is texture-based and lightweight for the MVP, now using a randomized seed profile for terrain kinds, a procedural terrain painter for visual variants/transitions, a runtime trail overlay, and separate runtime nature props that can block cells. Future per-cell interaction may still need Tilemap, mesh picking, or a dedicated map model/service.
- Camera and Build menu controls use direct Input System device reads. Future configurable controls should move to input actions.
- Build placement is runtime-only and creates construction sites that finalize into runtime building records. It does not yet create durable city state, save data, zoning, or long-term economy effects.
- House resources are runtime-only and local to each placed house. Garden Beds are installed for free by default and produce one assigned crop through resident work; Chicken Coop costs 1 Stone/2 Planks and passively produces Eggs; household foraging adds Berries/Roots/Mushrooms directly to the owning house; Householders can bring raw `Fish`/`Game` from Granaries, bring Pottery from Storage Yards, and cook ingredients plus 1 Pottery into recipe-based prepared dishes. Recipes currently span Poor/Common/Hearty/Fine/Feast quality tiers, and quality affects ration value and HUD/debug context. Nightly household dinner consumes prepared recipe dishes first before falling back to direct ingredients. There is no global economy, save data, storage cap, trade, spoilage, morale effect, or saved recipe state yet.
- Storage yard Logs, Stone, Iron, Coal, Clay, Planks, and Pottery are runtime-only, uncapped, and local to each placed storage yard; storage logistics feeds local construction reservations, production-input deliveries, householder Pottery pickup reservations, and food hauling into Granaries, but this still does not create a global economy or save data.
- Granary `Game` and `Fish` are runtime-only, uncapped, and local to each placed granary; food logistics centralizes production-building raw ingredient stock through shared Haulers, and Householders can move that stock into houses for cooking. Food is not spoiled, saved, traded, or exposed through a settlement-level stock service yet.
- Weather is runtime-only and currently affects visuals, ambience, wind, and water animation; it does not yet affect crops, movement, visibility, illness, fire risk, or save data.
- Generated Stone deposits are runtime-only map resource nodes mined by assigned stonecutters; starter-area deposits are guaranteed near the settlement spawn, but there is no Stone regrowth loop yet.
- Generated Iron fields are runtime-only map resource nodes for Mine worksites; they stay walkable but block normal building placement, are inspectable, avoid adjacent Coal fields, can be reserved/mined underground by hidden Miners only when under a Mine footprint, and currently feed Storage Yard Iron stock without construction costs or a global economy.
- Generated Coal fields are runtime-only map resource nodes for Coal Pit worksites; they stay walkable but block normal building placement, are inspectable, avoid adjacent Iron fields, can be reserved/mined by visible in-pit Coal Miners only when under a Coal Pit footprint, and currently feed Storage Yard Coal stock for Kiln input without construction costs or a global economy.
- Generated Clay fields are runtime-only near-water map resource nodes for Clay Pit worksites; they stay walkable, block normal building placement, are inspectable, avoid adjacent Iron/Coal/Clay fields, can be reserved/mined by visible Clay Diggers only when under a Clay Pit footprint, and currently feed Storage Yard Clay stock for Kiln input without construction costs or a global economy.
- Wildlife is runtime-only. Deer and birds are still ambient-only for the player; rabbits can be reserved and hunted by hunter-camp workers for local `Game`, with missed arrows releasing and startling the rabbit instead of creating a carcass; wolves are autonomous population-control predators that only hunt rabbit/deer surplus above high thresholds, chase the exact target position after reaching the target cell, and use a short fast pounce once close; current wildlife generation favors more numerous smaller groups over a few dense groups but no longer uses map-wide spawn, instead requiring daylight-range hidden near-settlement cells for initial spawn, reproduction birth cells, and migration targets; deer, rabbits, and wolves can swim across generated River cells only as transit toward a land target, do not treat Lake water as passable, and their land travel avoids placed buildings, active construction sites, and the campfire within a 4-cell structure buffer; lake fish can reproduce only inside hard-capped lake regions and per-shoal caps when a hidden near-settlement birth cell exists; river fish are non-reproductive pass-through agents spawned by a single timer from hidden near-settlement river-route cells; fish can be reserved and caught by fisher-hut workers from validated shore/land stand cells while the target remains in cast range; and wildlife still does not block walkability, reveal fog, save, or feed a global economy.
- Population is runtime-only. Residents currently move over trail-aware A* grid paths around camp/home and job targets, start as 3 finite starter-camp family blocks with adult children, move into houses after house construction based on home/family/pair state rather than job state, can migrate from parental homes into empty houses as adult children, can form simple opposite-gender household pairs with kinship checks where the wife takes the husband's family name, can have children inside occupied houses, consume nightly household dinners from prepared house `Dish` with per-resident malnutrition state, can forage home ingredients when unassigned and not householders, can fetch raw Granary food plus Storage Yard Pottery and cook stored ingredients plus Pottery as Householders, can die from annual age-based mortality with malnutrition multipliers and trigger runtime funerals/graves unless currently in funeral duty, can be manually assigned to work camps/storage yards as adults, can hunt rabbits through Hunter Camps, fish through Fisher Huts, and can be hired as Storage Yard builders for construction; assigned jobs remain assigned at night but new work cycles are paused outside Dawn-through-Dusk settlement work time, and housed idle residents sleep hidden inside homes during `Night` while notifying household dinner readiness. Residents do not have save data, explicit marriage UI, maiden-name display, or broader economy effects.
- Auto workforce assignment is runtime-only and can rebalance overstaffed auto-managed professions by unassigning surplus workers through normal worksite APIs; if no free adult is available, higher-scored shortages can also pull limited donors from lower-priority auto-managed roles. Normal surplus and demand donor releases only take idle worksite workers so active movement/work cycles are not interrupted; explicit priority-0 profession shutdowns can still cancel active workers. Worksite reads use a cached snapshot refreshed on a real-time cadence, successful assignments are capped per tick, only residents who are immediately idle are reused in the same tick, and workers returning carried resources re-enter the candidate pool on later ticks. It intentionally does not force-reassign busy home duty or funeral tasks.
- Construction resource costs are currently runtime-local and paid through Storage Yard Logs/Stone/Planks reservations plus loose construction piles; production stock must be hauled into Storage Yards before it can satisfy construction reservations. Carried construction resources dropped on resident death restore the original site's reservation when the site still needs that resource; late construction can require Planks, but no treasury, revenue, taxes, upkeep, or global stock service exists yet.
